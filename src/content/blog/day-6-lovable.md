Day 6 of 7 AIs I use as a PM:

Lovable.

The old product cycle was brutal.

Have an idea.

Write a doc.

Wait for design.

Wait for engineering.

Debate in meetings.

Build a version.

Realize the idea was unclear from the start.

AI prototyping tools are changing that.

Lovable lets you turn an idea into something users can actually click.

Fast.

That speed matters.

Because most product ideas don't need a full build first.

They need a reality check.

Does the flow make sense?

Does the value prop land?

Does the user know what to do next?

Is this actually useful or just clever?

A prototype answers those questions faster than a 10-page doc.

That doesn't mean PMs should bypass engineers.

It means PMs should show up with clearer thinking.

Instead of saying:

"I think this could work."

You can say:

"Here's a rough version. Let's react to this."

That changes the conversation.

The future PM is not just writing tickets.

They're making ideas tangible.

Fast.

Because speed to learning is becoming one of the biggest advantages in product.
